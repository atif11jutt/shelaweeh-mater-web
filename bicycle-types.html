11 Different Types of Bicycles

1.Road Bike:

The term road bicycle is used to describe bicycles built for traveling at speed on paved roads. Some sources use the term to mean racing bicycle.

2.Mountain Bike:

TA mountain bicycle is a bicycle designed for off-road cycling. Mountain bikes share some similarities with other bicycles, but incorporate features designed to enhance durability and performance in rough terrain.

3.Touring Bike:

These are almost like the traditional road bikes, except with a few tweaks and changes that make them perfect for long-distance bike tours.

4.Folding Bike:

Folding bikes are believed to be excellent travel companions. As the name suggests, they fold super easily and smoothly, allowing them to be able to effortlessly fit on a boat, in the trunk of a car or on a subway.

5.Track Bike:

Fixed Gear or Track bikes are primarily used for racers and athletes that are training for professional races.

6.BMX:

BMX, an abbreviation for bicycle motocross or bike motocross, is a cycle sport performed on BMX bikes, either in competitive BMX racing or freestyle BMX, or else in general on- or off-road recreation.

7.Recumbent Bike:

This type of bicycle is more popular as a “non-traditional” bicycle because it requires one to sit in a seat that is lower to the ground and looks like a chair.

8.Cyclocross Bike:

A cyclocross bike is a specific form of drop-bar bike that is built to tackle the popular racing discipline that is cyclocross.

9.Cruiser Bike:

Cruiser bikes are meant for leisurely rides that one would take around the town and their neighborhood.

10.Hybrid Bike:

This type of bicycle is more popular as a “non-traditional” bicycle because it requires one to sit in a seat that is lower to the ground and looks like a chair.

11.Electric Bike:

Electric bikes work by using an electric motor and battery to help you power your bike.
